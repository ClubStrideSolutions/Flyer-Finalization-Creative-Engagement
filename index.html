⏰ Time Check

This is your LAST chance to make changes before printing! Work efficiently.

20-Minute Sprint Instructions

  1. Minutes 1-10: Final Edits
    • Check all phone numbers and addresses one last time
    • Verify QR codes work (test with your phone)
    • Make sure text is readable (no tiny fonts!)
    • Confirm colors print well (not too light)
  2. Minutes 11-15: Partner Check
    • Trade flyers with another team
    • Look for: typos, unclear information, missing details
    • Give specific feedback: "The phone number is hard to read" not "It looks weird"
  3. Minutes 16-20: Submit for Printing
    • Make final corrections based on feedback
    • Save as PDF (best for printing)
    • Upload to shared folder
    • Fill out print request form (how many copies needed)
Quick Quality Checklist:
  • ✓ Organization name and logo visible
  • ✓ Contact info is correct and easy to find
  • ✓ Hours of operation included
  • ✓ "Free" or "No cost" clearly stated
  • ✓ Privacy/confidentiality mentioned
  • ✓ At least one visual element (icon, photo, or graphic)

What's a 30-Second Pitch?

A quick, natural way to introduce your flyer that covers:

  • Hook: Get their attention (5 seconds)
  • Value: Why they should care (10 seconds)
  • Details: Key info from flyer (10 seconds)
  • Action: What to do next (5 seconds)

Demo Instructions

Part 1: Pitch Prep (10 minutes)

Example Pitch Structure:
"Hey! (show flyer) Real quick – did you know you can get free STI testing without your parents knowing? (pause) This shows all the places in Vallejo where it's totally confidential and free. They even have Spanish speakers. (point to QR code) Scan this to find the closest one. Keep it in case you or a friend needs it!"
  • Write your pitch using the structure above
  • Practice with your team 2-3 times
  • Time yourself – aim for 25-35 seconds
  • Make it sound like YOU, not a robot!

Part 2: Group Demos (15 minutes)

  • Each team presents their pitch to the full group
  • Hold up your flyer so everyone can see
  • Speak clearly but conversationally
  • It's okay to be nervous – we're all learning!

Part 3: Peer Feedback (5 minutes)

Feedback Focus

After each pitch, share:

  • 🌟 One thing that worked well
  • 💡 One specific suggestion
  • ✅ Would you take the flyer? Why/why not?

Today's Focus: Pick ONE Strategy to Master

Don't try to do everything! Choose the strategy that feels most natural to you and your team.

Strategy 1: Stress Check Stickers

What you need: Sticker sheets (provided), resource cards

How it works:

  • Approach: "Hey, how's your day going?"
  • Offer sticker based on response
  • 😊 = "Keep it up!" + general wellness resource
  • 😟 = "Been there!" + mental health resource
  • 🍕 = "Same!" + food resource card

Practice focus: Natural conversation starters

Strategy 2: Myth Buster Challenge

What you need: True/False cards, resource flyers as "prizes"

How it works:

  • Set up at lunch table or hallway
  • Call out: "Quick health myth quiz – win free stuff!"
  • Ask one question: "True or false: You need parent permission for counseling?"
  • Correct answer = resource card + "You're right! Here's more info"

Practice focus: Making it fun, not preachy

Strategy 3: Wellness Playlist Exchange

What you need: Playlist cards, QR codes to resources

How it works:

  • Create cards with song suggestions for different moods
  • Approach: "Trading wellness playlists – what helps you chill?"
  • Exchange song ideas, give card with playlist + resource
  • Each mood playlist links to relevant support

Practice focus: Connecting through shared interests

30-Minute Practice Session

  1. Choose Your Strategy (5 minutes)
    • Pick ONE as a team
    • Consider your school culture – what would work best?
    • Think about YOUR comfort level
  2. Prepare Materials (10 minutes)
    • Gather supplies from resource table
    • Organize your materials in a way that's easy to carry
    • Make sure you have enough for practice
  3. Practice Rounds (15 minutes)
    • Pair up with another team
    • Take turns being "students" and "peer educators"
    • Run through your strategy 3 times each
    • Give feedback: What felt natural? What was awkward?
Remember: The goal is to make resources feel like friendly help, not homework! Keep it light, keep it real, and don't force conversations. Sometimes just leaving resources in bathroom stalls or study areas works too!
